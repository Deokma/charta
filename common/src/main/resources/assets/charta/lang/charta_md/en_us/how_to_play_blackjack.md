### **Objective:**

Get a hand value closer to **21** than the dealer without going over. All players compete against the dealer, not each other.

### **Setup:**

**Players:** 1–8 players vs. the Dealer

**Deck:** Standard 52-card deck (4 decks shuffled together)

- Each player starts with a configurable number of chips.
- A minimum bet is set before the session begins.

### **Card Values:**

- **2–10** — Face value (2 = 2 points, 9 = 9 points, etc.)
- **Jack, Queen, King** — 10 points each
- **Ace** — 11 points, or 1 if 11 would cause a bust

### **Game Flow:**

**1. Betting Phase**

All players place their bets simultaneously before any cards are dealt. Use the quick buttons (+10, +25, +50, +100) or type a custom amount.

**2. Deal**

Each player receives **2 face-up cards**. The dealer receives **1 face-up** and **1 face-down** (hole card).

**3. Player Turns** (left to right)

Each player chooses one of the following actions:

- **Hit** — Take one more card. You can hit as many times as you like.
- **Stand** — Keep your current hand and end your turn.
- **Double Down** — Double your bet and receive exactly **one more card**, then stand automatically. Only available on your first two cards.

If your total exceeds **21**, you **bust** and lose your bet immediately.

**4. Dealer's Turn**

After all players have acted, the dealer reveals the hole card:

- The dealer **must hit** on 16 or less.
- The dealer **must stand** on 17 or more.

**5. Payout**

| Result | Payout |
|--------|--------|
| Your hand beats dealer (no bust) | Win your bet (1:1) |
| **Blackjack** — Ace + 10-value card on first 2 cards | Win 1.5× your bet |
| Dealer busts, you don't | Win your bet (1:1) |
| Tie with dealer | **Push** — bet returned |
| Your hand loses or you bust | Lose your bet |

### **Blackjack:**

A **Blackjack** is an Ace plus any 10-value card (10, J, Q, K) dealt on the first two cards. It pays **3:2** (1.5× your bet). If the dealer also has Blackjack, it's a push.

### **Winning the Session:**

The game continues until only one player has chips remaining, or all players run out. The player with the most chips wins the session.
