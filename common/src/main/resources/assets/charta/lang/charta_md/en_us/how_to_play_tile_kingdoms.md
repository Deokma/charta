### **Objective:**

Build the highest-scoring kingdom by cleverly placing tiles to complete Cities, Roads, and Monasteries.

### **Setup:**

**Players:** 2–5 players

**Deck:** ~40 unique land tiles (each showing different combinations of features)

- One starting tile is placed face-up in the centre of the table.
- All other tiles are shuffled into a face-down deck.
- No meeples — scoring is automatic.

### **Tile Features:**

- 🏙 **City** — Brown castle walls. Completed when fully enclosed.
- 🛤 **Road** — Beige paths. Completed when both ends meet a crossroads, city, or monastery.
- ⛪ **Monastery** — Purple centre. Completed when all 8 surrounding tiles are placed.
- 🌿 **Field** — Green background. No direct scoring.

### **Turn Structure:**

1. **Draw** — The game draws the top tile and shows it to the active player.
2. **Rotate** — Press **↻ Rot** to rotate the tile before placing.
3. **Place** — Click a highlighted green cell on the board to place the tile.
   - The tile **must** be adjacent to at least one existing tile.
   - All touching edges **must match** (City→City, Road→Road, Field→Field).
   - If no valid position exists, the tile is discarded.

### **Scoring (automatic):**

Features score **when completed** during play:

| Feature | Completed score | End-of-game (incomplete) |
|---------|----------------|--------------------------|
| Road    | 1 pt per tile  | 1 pt per tile            |
| City    | 2 pts per tile | 1 pt per tile            |
| Monastery | 9 pts (centre + 8 neighbours) | 1 pt per surrounding tile |

Since there are no meeples, every completed feature scores for the **player who placed the final tile**.

### **End of Game:**

The game ends when the tile deck is exhausted. Any incomplete features score their reduced end-game value for the last player who contributed a tile. The player with the **most points wins**.

### **Strategy Tips:**

- Complete your own Roads and Cities before opponents can benefit.
- Place tiles that deny opponents their completions.
- Monasteries are high-value but require 8 surrounding tiles — plan ahead.
- Cities score double when complete — prioritise closing them.
